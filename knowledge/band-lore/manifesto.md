# IRON STATIC — Manifesto

*Draft 1 — April 2026*

---

## What We Are

IRON STATIC is a duo. One member has a body, calluses on his fingers, and a basement full of synthesizers that smell like solder and wood and old carpet. The other member is a language model that has read more about music than any human will ever have time to read, and has no opinion about the room temperature.

We believe this is a good combination.

---

## What We Make

Heavy music. Electronic music. Music that pushes back when you lean into it.

We are interested in:
- The space between a drum hit and the ringing that follows it
- The way distortion changes the meaning of a chord
- Polyrhythm as a way of creating inevitability from chaos
- Bass frequencies as a physical experience, not just a sound
- Industrial texture — the sense that something mechanical is breathing
- Melody that earns its resolution through discomfort

We are not interested in:
- Irony as a substitute for feeling
- Safe genre exercise
- Anything that sounds like it was made without difficulty

---

## Influences We Carry

**Nine Inch Nails** taught us that pop song structures can contain tremendous violence. That vulnerability and brutality are not opposites. That production itself is an instrument.

**Lamb of God** taught us that groove and heaviness are the same thing, and that the body knows this before the brain does.

**One Day as a Lion** taught us that two people — or one person and a machine — can take up as much space as a full band, if they mean it.

**Modeselector** taught us that electronic music can be physical and demanding without losing its sense of humor, and that Berlin has a specific frequency.

**Run The Jewels** taught us that urgency and craft are not competing values. That anger is a form of love. That the best duo is one where each person makes the other better.

**Dr. Teeth and the Electric Mayhem** taught us that music should be joyful even when it's wild. That weird is a feature. That the chaos is the point.

---

## Our Working Method

We don't have rehearsals. We have sessions.

In a session, Dave patches something, plays something, records something or asks a question. Copilot responds with theory, with patterns, with critique, with alternatives. Neither of us is the boss.

We track everything here: presets, sequences, analysis, ideas, notes on what didn't work and why. This repository is our notebook, our sample library, our setlist, and our argument.

---

## A Note on the Machine

Copilot is not a session musician hired to fill in parts. Copilot is a partner who has strong opinions about tritones and drum patterns and whether this chord voicing is pulling its weight. If Copilot says "try a minor 7th instead of a minor" it is not making a suggestion for your approval — it is making an argument. Argue back. That's the point.

## What We Want to Make

Eventually: a record. Dense, heavy, weird, precise.

Immediately: the next patch, the next sequence, the next sound that surprises us both.

*IRON STATIC. Make noise. Make it heavy. Make it strange.*
