### Track: Rev2-A → Wavetable
**Role**: Massive detuned pad wall. Climax only (bars 57–72). Sustained A minor chord.

**Oscillators**
- OSC 1: Classic Analog Saw table, position 0.0, Octave 0, Semi 0, Detune +0.20, Level 100%
- OSC 2: Classic Analog Saw table, position 0.0, Octave 0, Semi 0, Detune -0.20, Level 85%
- Sub Oscillator: On, Level 50%, Octave -1

**Unison**
- Voices: 8
- Amount: 0.35 (tight ensemble — not wide chorus, more like a slightly out-of-tune string section)
- Mode: Eco

**Filter 1** — Moog ladder LP model (PRD)
- Cutoff: 22%
- Resonance: 15%
- Drive: 15%
- Slope: 24dB

**Amp Envelope**
- Attack: 1.5s — pad swells into the Climax, not a hard entry
- Decay: 2.0s
- Sustain: 80%
- Release: 3.0s

**Filter Envelope → Filter 1 Cutoff**
- Attack: 2.0s
- Decay: 0
- Sustain: 100%
- Amount: +15% — filter opens as pad arrives, then holds fully open

**LFO 1 → OSC 1 + OSC 2 wavetable position**
- Rate: 0.07 Hz (one cycle ~14 seconds)
- Amount: 10% — slow harmonic drift, the pad breathes between positions

**FX Chain (post-device)**
1. Echo: Wobble 15%, Filter LP 3kHz, Feedback 35%, Repitch on, Dry/Wet 20%
2. Hybrid Reverb: Shimmer algorithm, mix 12%, Dark Hall IR underneath at 50% blend, Dry/Wet 30%

### Track: Rev2-B → Meld (Fold FM)
**Role**: Harmonic movement layer over the Wavetable static mass. Climax only (bars 57–72). Enters 4 bars after Rev2-A.

**Engine**: Fold FM

**Engine Parameters**
- Fold: 0.25 (starting point — modulated upward via LFO over Climax duration)
- FM Amount: 40% — sufficient FM density without destroying tonality
- Pitch: A root (same as Rev2-A — the two layers share the root, differ in harmonic character)

**Amp Envelope**
- Attack: 2.5s — enters later than Rev2-A, builds inside the Climax
- Decay: 0
- Sustain: 75%
- Release: 4.0s — longer tail than Rev2-A, so the two layers decay at different rates on cutoff

**LFO 1 → Fold Amount**
- Rate: 0.04 Hz (one cycle ~25 seconds — very slow, fold increases over the 16-bar Climax)
- Amount: 30% — by the end of the Climax, Fold is near 0.55, spectral density has roughly doubled

**FX Chain**
1. Roar: M-S mode, Stage 1 only, Soft Sine curve, Amount 35% — saturates center while leaving the Wavetable's wide sides intact
2. Hybrid Reverb: Tides algorithm, Dry/Wet 25% — gives Meld organic movement vs. Wavetable's static hall

### Track: Take5 → Drift
**Role**: Per-voice detuning, dissonant chord stabs on Drop downbeats (every 2 bars, bars 25–40). Energy 9/10.

**MIDI FX (before Drift)**
- Chord device: Shift1 = +1 (Bb — the Phrygian Dominant minor 2nd), Shift2 = +7 (E — perfect 5th), Velocity1 = 80%, Velocity2 = 95%
- Scale device: A Phrygian Dominant — safety net for any out-of-key notes

**Oscillator**
- Shape: Sawtooth, Octave 0

**Drift Amount**: 0.40 — significant per-voice drift for organic instability
**Drift Rate**: 0.25 Hz

**Filter**
- Type: LP
- Cutoff: 35%
- Resonance: 20%

**Amp Envelope** — this is the stab shape; Drift's detuning affects pitch, not transient
- Attack: 2ms (near zero — snap is essential at Energy 9/10)
- Decay: 350ms
- Sustain: 0%
- Release: 150ms

*Critic concern addressed*: Drift's per-voice detuning operates on pitch stability, not the amplitude transient. A 2ms attack envelope on a sawtooth oscillator through a 35% LP filter will land hard regardless of drift amount. The snap is there.

**LFO → Pan**
- Rate: 0.05 Hz, Amount: 15% — stabs drift slightly left/right each hit, never static in the field

**FX Chain**
1. Roar: Hard curve, 1 stage serial, Amount 40%, Dry/Wet 50% — the stab needs to punch through the Drop's full-energy drum/lead wall. Without this, Drift will be too clean.

### Track: Subharmonicon → Operator
**Role**: FM feedback drone, A + Bb half-step crunch, breathes. Frantic acceleration = Digitakt pattern change at bar 31, not from this device.

**Algorithm**: 7 — three modulators stacked into one carrier. Maximum harmonic density from a single pitch input.

**Carrier (Op A)**
- Frequency Ratio: 1.00
- Level: 100
- Feedback: 0 (carrier is clean — feedback goes on Op B below)
- Envelope: Attack 800ms, Decay 0, Sustain 100%, Release 2.0s

**Modulator 1 (Op B)**
- Frequency Ratio: 1.00
- Level: 70 — heavy modulation of carrier
- Feedback: 62 — above 50%, self-oscillation begins. This creates the drone's noise content and instability. This is the primary timbral character of the patch.
- Envelope: Same shape as carrier

**Modulator 2 (Op C)**
- Frequency Ratio: 2.00 (octave above)
- Level: 45
- Envelope: Attack 1.2s (slower to enter — adds harmonic complexity after the fundamental establishes)

**Modulator 3 (Op D)**
- Frequency Ratio: 0.50 (sub)
- Level: 35
- Envelope: Attack 600ms

**LFO → Modulator 1 Level**
- Rate: 0.08 Hz — the harmonic density breathes. When LFO dips, the carrier cleans up; when it peaks, the feedback roar increases. The drone is alive.
- Amount: 20 units

**MIDI Pitch Setup**
The Bb (♭II) is generated via Chord MIDI FX: Shift1 = +1 semitone. Play A, the chord device adds Bb. Both pitches feed into Operator simultaneously — the minor 2nd interval beats against the FM feedback, creating the "metal grinding" harmonic texture the brainstorm calls for.

**FX Chain**
1. Hybrid Reverb: Dark Hall, Pre-delay 45ms, Dry/Wet 35% — the drone needs space, but space that feels underground, not open
2. Echo: Tape Wobble 20%, Filter LP at 2kHz, Feedback 35%, Dry/Wet 25% — degraded tape echo on the drone tail

### Track: DFAM → Collision
**Role**: Industrial percussion. Clinical in Intro/Build/Drop (Energy 2–9). Destroyed in Breakdown (Energy 3 — but the Critic note says distortion chain must be explicit). Tuned to A.

**Mallet (Exciter)**
- Type: Noise
- Noise Amount: 65%
- Noise Color: 60% (slightly bright — metallic attack)
- Stiffness: 25% (not too stiff — wants smear on attack, not pure click)
- Velocity Sensitivity: 100%
- Volume: 85%

**Resonator**
- Type: Tube — per Sound Designer knowledge: "Tube and Plate at high resonance decay give metallic atonal percussion"
- Tune: A2 (root key)
- Decay: 1.8s — long ring, industrial metallic sustain
- Quality: 80%
- Volume: 100%

**Clinical FX Chain (Intro through Drop, bars 1–48)**
1. Utility: Bass Mono below 120Hz — keeps low end centered before distortion
2. EQ Eight: High shelf +2dB at 6kHz, notch at 400Hz (-3dB) — emphasize the metallic ring, reduce boxy resonance

**Destroyed FX Chain (Breakdown, bars 49–56) — automate as Rack macro**
Add an Instrument Rack. Put the Collision + clinical chain in chain 1. Add chain 2 as the destroyed path:
1. Saturator: Sinoid Fold curve, Drive 65%, Pre-EQ bass cut at 200Hz, Dry/Wet 100%
2. Amp: Heavy model, Gain 55%, Bass 40%, Mid 65%, Treble 35%
3. Cabinet: 4x12, on-axis dynamic mic

Automate the Rack Chain Selector: Chain 1 (0–63) = clinical, Chain 2 (64–127) = destroyed. Automate at bar 49 onset.

### Track: Minibrute2S → Analog
**Role**: Main lead voice for Tetanus Pulse Drop (bars 25–40) and Climax (bars 57–72). Must riff. Serrated, unstable, screaming, contained, sharp.

**OSC 1**
- Shape: Sawtooth
- Octave: 0
- Semi: 0
- Detune: +0.05 — very slight edge against other tracks in the mix
- Level: 100%

**OSC 2**: Off — mono lead, no OSC 2 mass

**Sub Oscillator**
- Octave: -1
- Level: 20% — hint of weight only, not defining the sound

**Filter 1** — High Pass (replaces Steiner-Parker HP behavior)
- Type: HP
- Frequency: 600Hz (~10 o'clock feel)
- Resonance: 65% — high HP resonance creates the sharp ringing peak. The Steiner-Parker HP has a distinctive resonance character; Analog's HP resonance is the closest in-box equivalent.
- Drive: 70% — this is the Brute Factor analog. High Amp Drive in Analog adds feedback-loop-style harmonic saturation.

**Filter 2** — Low Pass (high shelf, open)
- Type: LP
- Frequency: 8kHz
- Resonance: 0%

**Amp Envelope** — riff instrument, needs instant attack
- Attack: 0ms
- Decay: 120ms
- Sustain: 70%
- Release: 80ms

**Filter Envelope → Filter 1 Cutoff**
- Attack: 5ms
- Decay: 180ms
- Sustain: 0%
- Amount: +15% — filter pluck on each note onset. This is the "bite" that makes the riff feel alive rather than machine-flat.

**LFO 1 → Filter 1 Frequency**
- Rate: Sync to 1/16 at 95 BPM
- Amount: 8% — subtle filter movement synchronized to the beat. Adds the "unstable" quality without making the pitch waver.

**FX Chain**
1. Roar: Feedback Note mode (MIDI sidechain to input pitch), feedback amount 30%, Soft Sine stage — creates a tuned feedback resonance pitched to whatever note is playing. The lead sounds like it's on the edge of feeding back at every note.
2. Overdrive: Bandpass pre-drive at 2.5kHz, narrow bandwidth, Drive 40% — focused mid destruction that cuts through without adding low-end mud
3. Echo: Repitch mode on, Wobble 25%, Feedback 40%, Filter LP 4kHz, Dry/Wet 18% — degraded tape echo on the lead tail. Repitch means pitch changes mid-feedback like a slowing tape deck.

### Track: Pigments → Arturia Pigments VST3
**Role**: Granular scraping-metal texture bed, Breakdown (bars 49–56). "System failure" pitch drop at bar 56.

This is a hardware VST — parameters set inside the plugin, not from Ableton's device panel. Design brief for when you open it:

**Engine**: Sample engine
**Source sample**: Load a recording of scraping metal or a steel cable under tension. If none exists yet, use any available metal percussion sample from `audio/samples/` as starting point.

**Granular Settings**
- Grain size: 80ms
- Density: 4–6 grains/s (sparse — not a cloud, more like isolated metallic events)
- Position: Modulate slowly via LFO (rate 0.05 Hz) — scans through the sample, different textures each cycle
- Pitch spread: ±12 cents

**Filter**: HP at 300Hz (remove sub so Operator's drone owns the low end), LP at 10kHz

**Macro M1 → Master Pitch**: Automate down -12 semitones over bars 55–56 (the "system failure" drop the brainstorm describes). This is the transition into Climax.

**Macro M2 → Grain Density**: Automate from 4 to 16 grains/s as the pitch drops — more granular chaos as the system fails.

**FX inside Pigments**: Use the internal reverb, room size large, Dry/Wet 40%.
