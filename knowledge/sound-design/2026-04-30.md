## Elektron Digitakt MK1

### Preset 1: Fractured Concrete — Heavy industrial groove foundation

**Concept:** This is the core rhythmic driver, built from overdriven and bit-crushed one-shots. It provides the weight and industrial texture needed to anchor a track in the NIN or Lamb of God pocket.

**Synthesis Approach:**
- **Sample Source:** Load short, punchy, acoustic-adjacent samples for the kick and snare (e.g., a tight metal kick, a sharp piccolo snare). For hi-hats, use a sample of machine noise or static.
- **Filter:** Start with the filter wide open (LPF, FREQ=127) on the kick. On the snare, use a high-pass filter (HPF, FREQ=~40) to carve out low-end mud.
- **Envelope (AMP):** Very fast attack on all sounds. For the kick, set a medium hold and decay to give it body (ATK=0, HOLD=10, DEC=70). For the snare, a much faster decay to keep it snappy (ATK=0, HOLD=0, DEC=45).
- **Modulation:** Use the LFO to subtly modulate the sample start point on the hi-hat track to avoid machine-gun repetition. Set LFO1 to target `SAMPLE START`, use a random waveform, and keep the depth low (DEPTH=~5).
- **Effects:** Heavy use of the internal Overdrive on the kick and snare tracks. Send hi-hats to the Delay with a high feedback setting for trailing textures.

**Parameter Starting Points:**
1.  **[SRC] Kick Sample:** A short, dry 808-style or tight acoustic kick.
2.  **[AMP] Overdrive (Kick Track):** 75
3.  **[AMP] Bit Reduction (Snare Track):** 12-18 (to add grit and crunch)
4.  **[FILTER] Filter Freq (Snare Track):** 40 (HPF mode)
5.  **[LFO1] Destination (Hi-Hat Track):** `SAMPLE START`
6.  **[LFO1] Depth (Hi-Hat Track):** 5
7.  **[FX] Delay Send (Hi-Hat Track):** 60

**IRON STATIC Usage:**
-   **When to reach for this sound:** The primary rhythmic backbone for a heavy, driving section. This is the main beat.
-   **Digitakt integration hint:** Use MIDI tracks to sequence external synths, but use parameter locks on the audio tracks to trigger filter sweeps (CC 74) and LFO depth changes (CC 109) on the snare and hats for dynamic fills.
-   **One thing that makes it heavy or weird:** The combination of heavy overdrive on the kick and the subtle but noticeable bit-crushing on the snare gives the groove an aggressive, broken-machine texture.

### Preset 2: Unstable Drone Pad — Dissonant textural rhythmic source

**Concept:** This preset turns the Digitakt into a rhythmic drone machine, creating a tense, evolving pad from a single sample. It's designed to fill space with dissonant texture that can be sequenced like a percussive element.

**Synthesis Approach:**
- **Sample Source:** Load a long, harmonically rich but non-musical sample. Think field recordings of industrial machinery, bowed metal, or a detuned analog synth drone.
- **Filter:** Use a band-pass filter to isolate a specific resonant frequency in the sample. Start with a narrow resonance (Q) and sweep the frequency to find a sweet spot that rings out.
- **Envelope (AMP):** Instant attack, but a very long decay and full sustain (ATK=0, DEC=100, SUS=127, REL=80). This allows trigs to either be short stabs or long, overlapping notes.
- **Modulation:** Set LFO1 to modulate the filter cutoff frequency. Use a slow sine or triangle wave (RATE=~15-20, MULT=1x) and a moderate depth (DEPTH=~40) to create a slow, sweeping, siren-like quality.
- **Effects:** Drench the sound in the internal Reverb (large hall setting) to smear the texture into a background wash.

**Parameter Starting Points:**
1.  **[SRC] Sample:** A 10-15 second sample of metallic scraping or machine hum.
2.  **[FILTER] Filter Type:** Band-pass (BP).
3.  **[FILTER] Filter Freq:** 64 (to start, sweep to taste).
4.  **[FILTER] Filter Resonance:** 90.
5.  **[LFO1] Destination:** `FILTER FREQ`.
6.  **[LFO1] Rate:** 18.
7.  **[LFO1] Depth:** 42.
8.  **[FX] Reverb Send:** 100.

**IRON STATIC Usage:**
-   **When to reach for this sound:** Use as a background tension layer during a sparse intro or breakdown, or sequence it with a sparse 16th-note pattern to create a skittering, textural counter-rhythm to the main beat.
-   **Digitakt integration hint:** Dedicate a single audio track to this sound. Use parameter locks to change the LFO rate or the filter's base frequency on specific steps, creating a rhythmically evolving texture that never quite repeats.
-   **One thing that makes it heavy or weird:** By sequencing a sustained, dissonant sample and modulating its filter with an LFO, you create a pad that has a defined rhythmic pulse but a constantly shifting, unsettling harmonic character that fits perfectly with the E Phrygian mode of "Ignition Point."

## Sequential Rev2

### Preset 1: Corroded Choir — Dissonant, detuned, evolving, menacing texture

**Concept:** A bi-timbral pad that sounds like a choir of malfunctioning androids, slowly drifting in and out of tune. It's designed to fill the background with a sense of dread and harmonic instability, perfect for the E Phrygian mode of "Ignition Point."

**Synthesis Approach:**
- **Engine / oscillator setup:** Bi-timbral (Layer A + B). Layer A uses two detuned saw waves with 4-voice unison for a thick, swarming sound. Layer B uses a saw and a triangle+saw wave, detuned up a perfect fifth, with 2-voice unison to create a tense harmonic layer.
- **Filter starting point:** Layer A's Curtis 4-pole LP is mostly closed (cutoff ~40%) with moderate resonance. Layer B's filter is more open (cutoff ~70%) to let the upper harmonic tension cut through.
- **Envelope character:** Very slow attack on both VCA and Filter envelopes (~2-3 seconds), with a long decay and release (~5-7 seconds) to ensure it breathes and evolves.
- **Modulation:** A slow LFO sine wave subtly opens and closes the filter on both layers. The "OSC SLOP" parameter is essential, introducing organic pitch drift that makes the whole patch feel unstable and alive.
- **Effects:** A dark, spacious hall reverb from the onboard effects engine.

**Parameter Starting Points:**
- `OSC SLOP`: 45
- `UNISON VOICES`: Layer A=4, Layer B=2
- `UNISON DETUNE`: Layer A=60
- `LAYER B TUNE`: +7 (for a tense fifth)
- `VCA ENV ATTACK`: 100
- `VCA ENV RELEASE`: 110
- `FILTER ENV AMOUNT`: +35
- `LFO 1 FREQ`: 15 (very slow, modulating filter cutoff)

**IRON STATIC Usage:**
- **When to reach for this sound:** Use for intros, breakdowns, or as a static atmospheric bed under a heavy rhythmic section. Holding a single low E root note will generate a full, evolving soundscape.
- **Digitakt integration hint:** Sequence long, overlapping notes on MIDI channels 2 (Layer A) and 3 (Layer B). Use the Digitakt's MIDI LFOs to automate `FILTER CUTOFF` (CC74) and `RESONANCE` (CC71) independently on each layer, creating a disorienting, polyrhythmic modulation.
- **One thing that makes it heavy or weird:** The weirdness comes from the combination of `OSC SLOP` and heavy unison detune, making the pitch feel broken and unreliable. The heaviness is the sheer density of the stacked, detuned oscillators.

### Preset 2: Phrygian Stab — Aggressive, percussive, syncopated, resonant, mean

**Concept:** A tight, punchy polyphonic stab for writing aggressive, syncopated chordal riffs. It's designed to cut through a dense mix and function like a rhythm guitar, providing the harmonic backbone for a groove-metal-inspired section.

**Synthesis Approach:**
- **Engine / oscillator setup:** Single layer. OSC 1 is a saw wave, OSC 2 is a square wave an octave down. This provides both a sharp edge and a solid fundamental.
- **Filter starting point:** Curtis 4-pole LP with a low cutoff (~35%) but very high resonance (~80%). This creates a sharp, vocal-like "yak" at the start of the note.
- **Envelope character:** Instant attack, fast decay (~250ms), zero sustain, and a very short release. The filter envelope has the same shape but with a high modulation amount, creating the percussive attack.
- **Modulation:** Velocity is mapped to both Filter Envelope Amount and VCA amplitude. This makes the patch highly responsive to playing dynamics — softer hits are muffled, harder hits scream.
- **Effects:** Onboard distortion, mixed in at about 30% wet to add grit and harmonic saturation without turning the sound to mush.

**Parameter Starting Points:**
- `OSC 2 FREQ`: -12 semitones
- `OSC MIX`: 90 (favoring the saw wave)
- `FILTER CUTOFF`: 40
- `FILTER RESONANCE`: 100
- `FILTER ENV AMOUNT`: +90
- `VCA ENV DECAY`: 60
- `VCA ENV SUSTAIN`: 0
- `EFFECTS TYPE 1`: Distortion

**IRON STATIC Usage:**
- **When to reach for this sound:** This is a primary riff-writing tool. Use it for tight, 16th-note patterns that lock in with the kick and bass, providing the main harmonic and rhythmic drive of a heavy section.
- **Digitakt integration hint:** Program a syncopated chord pattern on MIDI channel 2. Use parameter locks on velocity to create a strong accent pattern. For extra emphasis, p-lock `FILTER CUTOFF` (CC74) up by ~20-30 on specific hits you want to scream.
- **One thing that makes it heavy or weird:** The heaviness comes from the aggressive, resonant filter envelope creating a metallic, percussive attack. Playing E Phrygian chord voicings (like E-F-A or G-A-C) makes it sound instantly menacing and harmonically weird.

## Sequential Take 5

### Preset 1: Phrygian Tension Stab — Dissonant, percussive, syncopated, anxious

**Concept:** A detuned, percussive chord stab designed to carry harmonic tension. It functions like a pitched drum, providing a rhythmic, metallic punctuation that locks into the groove and defines the song's anxious E Phrygian feel.

**Synthesis Approach:**
-   **Engine / oscillator setup:** Two sawtooth waves. OSC 1 at root pitch. OSC 2 tuned up +6 semitones (a tritone) to create instant, unresolved dissonance. Add a small amount of OSC 2 -> OSC 1 FM for a clangorous, metallic texture.
-   **Filter starting point:** 4-pole low-pass filter. Cutoff set low (~35%), Resonance high (~70%).
-   **Envelope character:** Very plucky. Filter Env: instant attack, ~150ms decay, no sustain, high envelope amount. Amp Env: instant attack, ~200ms decay, no sustain, short release.
-   **Modulation:** LFO 1 (triangle wave) synced to 1/8 notes, subtly modulating the filter cutoff to create a gentle rhythmic pulse.
-   **Effects:** A small amount of onboard chorus to add width, but keep it tight and focused.

**Parameter Starting Points:**
1.  `OSC 2 PITCH`: +6 semitones
2.  `OSC MIX`: 50/50
3.  `FILTER CUTOFF` (CC 33): 45
4.  `FILTER RESONANCE` (CC 34): 90
5.  `FILT ENV AMT`: +85
6.  `FILT ENV DECAY`: 160ms
7.  `LFO1 FREQ` (CC 75): Set to 1/8 sync
8.  `LFO1 DESTINATION`: Filter Cutoff, Amount (CC 76) set to 15

**IRON STATIC Usage:**
-   **When to reach for this sound:** Use for syncopated, off-beat chord stabs during verses or as the main rhythmic element in a sparse, tense breakdown.
-   **Digitakt integration hint:** Sequence on MIDI track 4 (ch 4). Use parameter locks on `Filter Cutoff` (CC 33) to open and close the filter on specific steps, creating a dynamic, evolving pattern.
-   **One thing that makes it heavy or weird:** The built-in tritone interval between oscillators makes any chord played instantly dissonant and aggressive, perfectly fitting the Phrygian mode's character.

### Preset 2: Unstable Scream Lead — Chaotic, searing, breaking, raw

**Concept:** An overdriven, unstable monophonic lead that feels like it's constantly on the verge of breaking apart. This is for raw, high-energy moments where the melody needs to cut through with pure industrial aggression.

**Synthesis Approach:**
-   **Engine / oscillator setup:** Switch to Unison mode with 5 voices stacked. Two sawtooth waves with significant detuning between them. Turn the "Vintage" knob up to ~75% to introduce more random slop and instability.
-   **Filter starting point:** 4-pole low-pass. Cutoff at ~50%, Resonance cranked to ~85% to get it close to self-oscillation.
-   **Envelope character:** Fast attack, full sustain, medium release. Designed for held notes and fast legato runs.
-   **Modulation:** LFO 1 (sample & hold or random wave) set to a very fast, near-audio rate. Route it with a tiny amount to OSC 2's pitch to create a chaotic, gritty texture instead of a clean tone.
-   **Effects:** Onboard overdrive effect set high (~80%) and a short, dark reverb to give it space without washing it out.

**Parameter Starting Points:**
1.  `UNISON`: On, 5 voices
2.  `OSC 2 DETUNE`: +15 cents
3.  `VINTAGE KNOB`: 75%
4.  `FILTER CUTOFF` (CC 33): 60 (map to Mod Wheel for sweeps)
5.  `FILTER RESONANCE` (CC 34): 110
6.  `LFO1 FREQ` (CC 75): ~40 Hz (not synced)
7.  `LFO1 DESTINATION`: OSC 2 Pitch, Amount (CC 76) set to 5
8.  `DRIVE`: 80%

**IRON STATIC Usage:**
-   **When to reach for this sound:** Use for aggressive arpeggios, chaotic noise fills, or a high-register lead line in a chorus or bridge.
-   **Digitakt integration hint:** Sequence on track 4 (ch 4). Automate `FX Depth/Mix` (CC 18) to bring the reverb in and out, creating dramatic pushes and pulls in the mix. Use the Digitakt's arpeggiator for frantic, machine-gun style patterns.
-   **One thing that makes it heavy or weird:** The fast, random LFO modulating the oscillator pitch creates a sputtering, unstable "broken circuit" sound that prevents the lead from ever feeling safe or clean.

## Moog Subharmonicon

### Preset 1: Tectonic Hum — Deep, grinding, foundational, menacing drone

**Concept:** This is the bedrock of the track's low-end pressure. It provides a massive, slowly shifting drone rooted in E, with detuned subharmonics creating a sense of physical weight and dread.

**Synthesis Approach:**
- **Engine / oscillator setup:** VCO 1 is tuned to a low E. VCO 2 is detuned slightly sharp from VCO 1 to create a slow, beating phase. The subharmonics are set to low integer divisions to create a solid, consonant foundation that can be twisted with the filter.
- **Filter starting point:** Moog Ladder LP, cutoff set very low (~150Hz) to start, with moderate resonance (~40-50%) to add a woody growl when pushed.
- **Envelope character:** VCA Decay is maxed for an infinite drone. VCF Decay is also set very long to allow for slow, gradual filter movements.
- **Modulation:** The primary modulation is a very slow sequencer (e.g., /16 or /32 division) assigned to the VCF cutoff, creating a gradual, multi-bar sweep that feels architectural and immense.
- **Effects:** Best paired with an external, heavy saturation effect (like Ableton's Roar) and a dark, cavernous reverb.

**Parameter Starting Points:**
- `VCO 1 FREQ`: Tuned to E1
- `VCO 2 FREQ`: Fine-tuned just slightly sharp of VCO 1
- `SUB 1` & `SUB 2` (VCO 1): Set to `/4` and `/5`
- `CUTOFF`: ~150Hz
- `RESONANCE`: 4-5
- `VCA DECAY`: 10 (Max)
- `SEQ 1 ASSIGN`: VCF
- `RHYTHM 1`: `/16`

**IRON STATIC Usage:**
- **When to reach for this sound:** Use as the foundational drone in a heavy chorus or a sparse, tense breakdown. It fills the sub-bass spectrum and creates a sense of inevitability.
- **Digitakt integration hint:** Send clock to channel 5. Use a Digitakt MIDI LFO to slowly modulate the Subharmonicon's main Tempo knob, causing the filter sweep to subtly drift against the grid.
- **One thing that makes it heavy or weird:** The sheer low-frequency pressure is its weight. The weirdness comes from the slow, almost imperceptible filter sweep that evolves over 8 or 16 bars, making the foundation feel alive and unstable.

### Preset 2: Unstable Orbit — Chaotic, nervous, unstable, rhythmic chatter

**Concept:** A frantic, polyrhythmic sequence that feels like a machine on the verge of breaking. It creates a bed of high-frequency, interlocking chatter that sits above the main groove, adding tension and complexity.

**Synthesis Approach:**
- **Engine / oscillator setup:** VCO 1 is tuned to E4. VCO 2 is tuned to F4, the dissonant minor second of the E Phrygian scale. The subharmonics are set to high, prime-number divisions (/11, /13, etc.) to create strange, inharmonic overtones rather than a clear pitch.
- **Filter starting point:** Cutoff is set high (~2kHz) to let the chaotic frequencies through. Resonance is also high (60-70%) to give the filter a "pinging" quality that accentuates the percussive nature of the sound.
- **Envelope character:** VCA Decay is set very short for a plucky, staccato sound. VCF decay is also very short, creating a sharp, percussive click for each note.
- **Modulation:** Route SEQ 1 to the pitch of VCO 1 and SEQ 2 to the pitch of VCO 2. Set their Rhythm knobs to conflicting divisions (e.g., `/5` and `/7`) so they constantly trip over each other, generating a complex, non-repeating pattern.
- **Effects:** A fast, choppy delay or a gated reverb will accentuate the rhythmic chaos and add to the feeling of system malfunction.

**Parameter Starting Points:**
- `VCO 1 FREQ`: Tuned to E4
- `VCO 2 FREQ`: Tuned to F4
- `VCA DECAY`: 2-3 (very short)
- `CUTOFF`: ~2kHz
- `RESONANCE`: 6-7
- `RHYTHM 1`: `/5`
- `RHYTHM 2`: `/7`
- `SEQ 1 ASSIGN`: VCO 1
- `SEQ 2 ASSIGN`: VCO 2

**IRON STATIC Usage:**
- **When to reach for this sound:** Layer this in during an intro or bridge to build tension and create a feeling of instability before a heavy drop. It works well when contrasted against a very solid, simple drum beat.
- **Digitakt integration hint:** Send clock on channel 5. Patch the Subharmonicon's VCF CV output to the VCA CV input. This makes the filter cutoff control the volume, creating unpredictable rhythmic dynamics as the sequencers modulate the filter.
- **One thing that makes it heavy or weird:** The rhythmic friction between the `/5` and `/7` patterns combined with the harmonic clash of E and F makes it profoundly unsettling. It's the sound of two systems fighting for control.

## Moog DFAM

### Preset 1: Ignition Anvil — Sharp metallic industrial percussive clang

**Concept:** This sound is a high-resonance, metallic clang designed to cut through a dense mix like a hammer strike. It fills the need for non-traditional, industrial percussion that adds texture and aggression without occupying the kick or snare space.

**Synthesis Approach:**
- **Engine / oscillator setup:** Use both VCOs. VCO 1 is the primary sound source (Square wave) with Hard Sync enabled. VCO 2 (Triangle wave) is tuned much higher to modulate VCO 1's frequency, creating harsh, metallic, inharmonic overtones.
- **Filter starting point:** High VCF Peak (Resonance) to create a distinct ringing tone. VCF Cutoff is modulated by a fast envelope to create a sharp "ping" on the transient.
- **Envelope character:** VCF and VCA envelopes are set to Fast mode. Both have near-instant attacks and very fast decays (VCA decay slightly faster than VCF decay) to create a tight, percussive hit that doesn't wash out the groove.
- **Modulation:** The key is the interaction between the two VCOs via Hard Sync. The sequencer's Velocity output is patched to the VCF Cutoff to add dynamic variation to the brightness of each hit.
- **Effects:** Best used dry initially, then sent to a dirty, gated reverb in Ableton to give it a sense of space without muddying the rhythm.

**Parameter Starting Points:**
- **VCO 1 Waveform:** Square
- **VCO 1 Hard Sync:** On
- **VCO 2 Freq:** ~2-3 o'clock (tuned high to create clangorous sidebands)
- **VCF Peak:** ~3 o'clock (just before self-oscillation)
- **VCF EG Amount:** ~2 o'clock
- **VCA Decay:** ~9 o'clock (very short)
- **Sequencer Velocity (row 2):** Varied pattern to create rhythmic accents
- **Patchbay:** VELOCITY OUT → VCF CUTOFF IN

**IRON STATIC Usage:**
- **When to reach for this sound:** Use as a syncopated hi-hat or percussion layer in a heavy chorus or drop. It can carry the rhythm when the main drums are simplified, adding industrial urgency.
- **Digitakt integration hint:** Sequence from the Digitakt on MIDI channel 6. Automate CCs mapped to VCO 2 Frequency to change the metallic "pitch" of the clang, creating evolving, chaotic patterns.
- **One thing that makes it heavy or weird:** The Hard Sync between a low and a high oscillator creates an aggressive, non-tonal sound that feels inherently mechanical and abrasive.

### Preset 2: Pressure Pulse — Overdriven sub-bass rhythmic drone

**Concept:** A low-frequency, rhythmic pulse that blurs the line between a sub-bass and a kick drum. It provides foundational weight and a sense of relentless, mechanical pressure, perfect for intros or breakdowns.

**Synthesis Approach:**
- **Engine / oscillator setup:** Both VCOs tuned low, set to Square waves for maximum harmonic content. Slightly detune VCO 2 from VCO 1 to create a slow, thick beating effect. Drive the filter by turning up VCO 1, VCO 2, and Noise/VCA EG levels in the mixer.
- **Filter starting point:** VCF Cutoff set low (~9-10 o'clock) to keep it in the sub-bass territory. Moderate VCF Peak (~11 o'clock) to add a focused resonant bump that can be felt.
- **Envelope character:** VCA and VCF envelopes on Fast mode. Instant attack, with a medium decay (~11 o'clock) to create a solid "thump" that has body but doesn't linger too long.
- **Modulation:** The sequencer's Pitch output is patched to the VCF EG Amount. This makes some steps open the filter more than others, creating a rhythmic filter pattern on top of the note pattern.
- **Effects:** Add Roar or another saturator in Ableton to control the overdrive and shape the harmonic character of the low end.

**Parameter Starting Points:**
- **VCO 1/2 Freq:** Both around 8-9 o'clock (in the low range)
- **VCO 1/2 Waveform:** Square
- **Mixer Levels (VCO 1, VCO 2, Noise):** All at ~2-3 o'clock to overdrive the filter input
- **VCF Cutoff:** ~9 o'clock
- **VCA Decay:** ~11 o'clock
- **Sequencer Pitch (row 1):** Set a few steps high and most low to create the filter rhythm.
- **Tempo:** ~10 o'clock (slow)
- **Patchbay:** PITCH OUT → VCF EG AMT IN

**IRON STATIC Usage:**
- **When to reach for this sound:** Use it as the main rhythmic element in a sparse intro, or layer it under a 4/4 kick during a drop to add immense weight and a polyrhythmic feel.
- **Digitakt integration hint:** Trigger the DFAM sequencer to start/stop from the Digitakt, but let the internal 8-step sequence run. This creates an evolving 8-step pattern against the Digitakt's grid, a classic polyrhythmic technique.
- **One thing that makes it heavy or weird:** Driving the mixer hard into the low-passed ladder filter creates a warm, powerful saturation that feels physically heavy, while the self-modulation from the sequencer adds an evolving, machine-like unpredictability.

## Arturia Minibrute 2S

### Preset 1: Corroded Cog — Grinding, rhythmic, mechanical, bass, sequence

**Concept:** This is a percussive, sequenced bassline that feels like a massive, rusted gear turning over. It's designed to provide both the fundamental low-end and a layer of industrial, rhythmic grime that locks in with a heavy drum groove.

**Synthesis Approach:**
- **Engine / oscillator setup:** VCO1 on Sawtooth with the Metalizer at 50% to add metallic harmonics. VCO2 on Square wave, detuned by -10 cents for thickness. Mixer balances VCO1 (70%) and VCO2 (50%), with a touch of White Noise (15%).
- **Filter starting point:** Steiner-Parker filter in Low-Pass (LP) mode. Cutoff set low (around 9 o'clock / 80 Hz) with Resonance high (around 3 o'clock / 75%) to create a squelchy, aggressive edge on each note.
- **Envelope character:** Amp Env has an instant attack, 100ms decay, zero sustain, and a short 50ms release. This creates a tight, plucky sound for each sequencer step.
- **Modulation:** Use the sequencer's velocity output patched to the Metalizer input. This makes louder steps have more metallic bite, adding rhythmic variation to the texture.
- **Effects:** The Brute Factor knob is the main effect, set to about 60% to introduce feedback and heavy saturation without self-oscillating uncontrollably.

**Parameter Starting Points:**
1.  **VCO 1 Waveform:** Sawtooth
2.  **VCO 1 Metalizer:** 50%
3.  **VCO 2 Waveform:** Square
4.  **VCO 2 Fine Tune:** -10 cents
5.  **Filter Mode:** LP
6.  **Filter Resonance:** 75%
7.  **Amp Env Decay:** 100ms
8.  **Brute Factor:** 60%

**IRON STATIC Usage:**
- **When to reach for this sound:** Use as the main riff carrier in a verse or bridge, where the groove needs to be heavy and relentless. Perfect for a 16-step sequence in E Phrygian.
- **Digitakt integration hint:** Sequence from the Digitakt on MIDI channel 7. Use a CC parameter lock on CC74 (Filter Cutoff) to open the filter on specific steps for emphasis, creating a "wub" effect that accents the beat.
- **One thing that makes it heavy or weird:** The combination of the Metalizer's wave-folding clang and the Brute Factor's feedback distortion gives the sound a physically broken, overloaded character that goes beyond simple saturation.

### Preset 2: Failed Sync Lead — Dissonant, screaming, unstable, piercing, siren

**Concept:** A screaming lead sound designed for maximum tension and discomfort. It leverages oscillator sync and a high-pass filter to cut through the mix with a sound that feels like a system alarm or a machine tearing itself apart.

**Synthesis Approach:**
- **Engine / oscillator setup:** Engage Oscillator Sync. VCO1 (master) on Sawtooth, tuned low. VCO2 (slave) on a Triangle wave, tuned two octaves higher. The magic comes from sweeping the VCO2 pitch knob.
- **Filter starting point:** Steiner-Parker filter in High-Pass (HP) mode. Cutoff is set high (around 2 o'clock / 2 kHz) to shave off all body and leave only the piercing harmonics. Resonance is moderate (50%) to emphasize the sweep.
- **Envelope character:** Amp Env has a very fast attack, long decay (2-3 seconds), and high sustain. This allows notes to be held and swell into feedback.
- **Modulation:** Patch the AD Envelope output to the VCO2 Pitch input. Set the AD envelope with a fast attack and a 1-second decay. This creates a "zap" or "scream" at the start of each note as the pitch sweeps down rapidly.
- **Effects:** No Brute Factor. The clean, piercing nature of the sync is the goal here. External delay/reverb in Ableton is highly recommended.

**Parameter Starting Points:**
1.  **VCO Sync:** On
2.  **VCO 1 Waveform:** Sawtooth
3.  **VCO 2 Waveform:** Triangle
4.  **VCO 2 Pitch:** +24 semitones (or higher)
5.  **Filter Mode:** HP
6.  **Filter Cutoff:** 70%
7.  **AD Env Decay:** ~1 second
8.  **Patch:** AD Env Out → VCO 2 Pitch In

**IRON STATIC Usage:**
- **When to reach for this sound:** Ideal for held, dissonant notes over a chorus or as a tension-building element in an intro/outro. Lean into the F natural of the E Phrygian scale for maximum harmonic clash.
- **Digitakt integration hint:** Send notes on channel 7. Automate the CC for VCO2 Pitch from the Digitakt. A slow LFO assigned to this CC can create a continuous, evolving siren effect that feels unnerving and alive.
- **One thing that makes it heavy or weird:** Using the high-pass filter *removes* the fundamental weight, but makes the sound *feel* heavier by focusing all its energy into a narrow, psychoacoustically aggressive frequency band that demands the listener's attention.

## pigments

Here are two preset ideas for Arturia Pigments, tailored for the active song "Ignition Point."

### Preset 1: Corroded Sawtooth — Grinding, rhythmic, unstable, digital, rust

**Concept:** A detuned, sequenced bass/lead that feels like a rusty machine struggling to maintain a rhythm. It provides the core industrial texture and rhythmic drive for a heavy, repetitive riff.

**Synthesis Approach:**
- **Engine / oscillator setup**: Engine 1: Analog, Sawtooth wave, 3 unison voices, 15% detune. Engine 2: Wavetable, `Building Blocks > Formant II`, unison off. Engine 2 is tuned down one octave (-12 st) and mixed in at -8dB.
- **Filter starting point**: Filter 1 is a dirty Low Pass filter (try the Matrix-12 topology). Cutoff starts around 400Hz with Resonance at 0.6. Add 6dB of drive.
- **Envelope character**: VCA Env: instant attack, 400ms decay, zero sustain, 100ms release. Filter Env: instant attack, 250ms decay, 25% sustain, 50ms release.
- **Modulation**: LFO1 (synced, 1/8th note Saw Down shape) modulates the master tune of Engine 1 very slightly (+/- 5 cents) for a seasick pitch effect. Also route LFO1 to the Wavetable Position of Engine 2.
- **Effects**: A touch of Distortion (Tape Saturation) followed by a simple Reverb (Plate, 20% wet).

**Parameter Starting Points:**
- Engine 1 Detune: 15%
- Engine 2 Wavetable: `Building Blocks > Formant II`
- Filter 1 Cutoff (CC74): 400 Hz
- Filter 1 Resonance (CC71): 0.6
- Filter 1 Drive: 6.0 dB
