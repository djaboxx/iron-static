# Scales & Modes — IRON STATIC Reference

A practical reference filtered through the lens of heavy electronic music. For each scale: what it sounds like, when to use it, and how to implement it on the rig.

---

## The Core Palette

### Natural Minor (Aeolian)
**Formula**: 1 2 ♭3 4 5 ♭6 ♭7  
**In E minor**: E F# G A B C D  
**Character**: Dark, melancholic, familiar — the foundation.  
**Use when**: Most everything. Default starting point for IRON STATIC parts.  
**On Rev2**: Standard minor chord voicings. Minor 7ths, minor 9ths.  
**On Digitakt**: Bass sequences stay on E, G, A, B for maximum weight.

---

### Phrygian
**Formula**: 1 ♭2 ♭3 4 5 ♭6 ♭7  
**In E Phrygian**: E F G A B C D  
**Character**: Dark, tense, ancient-feeling — the flat-2 is threatening.  
**Use when**: Aggressive riff sections, tension builds, anything that needs an edge.  
**Key move**: The ♭II chord (F major over E bass) is the defining Phrygian cadence — heavy.  
**On Rev2**: F major chord over E bass note (Minibrute 2S) = classic Phrygian power move.

---

### Phrygian Dominant
**Formula**: 1 ♭2 3 4 5 ♭6 ♭7  
**In E Phrygian Dom**: E F G# A B C D  
**Character**: Exotic, Middle Eastern, threatening with a raised 3rd.  
**Use when**: Lead lines over a Phrygian riff; when you want the scale to feel dangerous AND melodic.  
**Note**: The major 3rd over the ♭2 creates maximum harmonic tension.

---

### Locrian
**Formula**: 1 ♭2 ♭3 4 ♭5 ♭6 ♭7  
**In B Locrian**: B C D E F G A  
**Character**: Maximum instability — the tonic chord is diminished, the scale has no stable root.  
**Use when**: Complete breakdown sections, abstract transitional material, maximum disorientation.  
**Caution**: Hard to sustain — use for effect, not as a key center for a whole song.

---

### Dorian
**Formula**: 1 2 ♭3 4 5 6 ♭7  
**In A Dorian**: A B C D E F# G  
**Character**: Minor with a raised 6th — darker than major, less heavy than Aeolian. Hopeful-dark.  
**Use when**: Melodic lead lines that need to cut through a heavy texture without losing darkness.  
**On Take 5 or Rev2**: Layer A Aeolian rhythm part + Layer B Dorian melody = interesting tension.

---

### Diminished (Octatonic)
**Formula**: 1 2 ♭3 4 ♭5 ♭6 6 7 (alternating W-H or H-W)  
**Character**: Symmetric, unsettling, machine-like — no clear tonal center.  
**Use when**: Industrial textures, chromatic movement, anything that should sound like machinery.  
**Key property**: Repeats every minor 3rd — E diminished = G diminished = B♭ diminished.

---

### Whole Tone
**Formula**: 1 2 3 ♯4 ♯5 ♯6 (all whole steps)  
**Character**: Floating, dreamlike, unresolved — Debussy / sci-fi.  
**Use when**: Transitional sections, intros, ambient interludes, anything that needs to sound untethered.  
**On Subharmonicon**: Tune VCO intervals to whole-tone ratios for subharmonic whole-tone drones.

## Modal Chord Progressions for Heavy Electronic Music

### Phrygian Heavy (E Phrygian)
```
Em — F — Em — F — Dm — C — Em
```
The ♭II (F) move is the hook. Use this as the main riff.

### Aeolian Dark (E minor)
```
Em — C — G — D      (VI-VII-I movement)
Em — Am — Em — Bm   (i–iv–i–v)
Em — G — D — Am     (classic dark minor loop)
```

### Dorian Melodic (A Dorian)
```
Am7 — G — F#m7♭5 — E  (the raised 6th F# creates the Dorian color)
Am — G — Am — Bm
```

## Keys for the Rig

Preferred root keys for IRON STATIC — naturally suited to the instruments:

| Key | Why |
|---|---|
| **E minor** | Guitar-adjacent; Digitakt bass sits heavy on E; Rev2 chord voicings feel natural |
| **A minor** | Comfortable for both Rev2 and Take 5; Subharmonicon drones easily |
| **D minor** | Slightly darker than A minor; good for slower, heavier material |
| **B minor** | Tense; pairs well with Phrygian progressions |
| **F# minor** | Bright-dark; good for driving, more aggressive parts |
