### 1. Song Idea

This is a structural and thematic evolution of the active track, "Rust Protocol."

*   **Working Title:** "Tetanus Pulse"
*   **Mood:** Jagged, Corrosive, Inevitable.
*   **Key/Scale:** A Phrygian Dominant. Rationale: The established A Phrygian root gives us the heavy, grounded feeling. Introducing the major third (C#) creates a severe harmonic tension against the flat second (Bb). This is the sound of metal grinding; it is the core harmonic concept of corrosion.
*   **Tempo Range:** 92–98 BPM.
*   **Featured Instruments:** Minibrute 2S (abrasive lead), DFAM (atonal metallic percussion), Pigments (granular texture bed).
*   **Unexpected Element:** The entire track's sense of space will be defined by a single, massive one-shot sample on Digitakt track 8: a 10-second recording of a steel I-beam being dropped on concrete. This track will only trigger once at the start of the song, but its reverb and delay sends will be automated throughout the arrangement, creating a decaying, ghost-like environment for the other elements to exist within. This directly addresses our interest in "the space between a drum hit and the ringing that follows it."

### 2. Arrangement Blueprint

This blueprint is for "Tetanus Pulse" as a B-section or new movement for "Rust Protocol." Total duration: 80 bars.

*   **Intro (8 bars):**
    *   **Dominant Instruments:** The "I-beam" sample's reverb tail (from Digitakt track 8 sends), a dry DFAM sequence panned hard left.
    *   **Energy Level:** 2/10.
    *   **Transition:** A slow, 4-bar filter sweep opens up on the DFAM sequence, revealing its high-frequency content.

*   **Build (16 bars):**
    *   **Dominant Instruments:** The Digitakt enters with the core kick/snare pattern. The Subharmonicon fades in with a low, two-note drone based on A and Bb.
    *   **Energy Level:** 5/10.
    *   **Transition:** The Subharmonicon's sequence rate doubles for the last 2 bars, creating a frantic rhythmic acceleration before cutting to silence on the last 8th note.

*   **Drop (16 bars):**
    *   **Dominant Instruments:** The full Digitakt beat hits. The Minibrute 2S enters with the main, distorted lead riff. The Take 5 plays a dissonant, high-voicing chord stab on the downbeat of every second bar.
    *   **Energy Level:** 9/10.
    *   **Transition:** A manual high-pass filter sweep on the Digitakt's main output removes all low-end over 4 bars, leaving only the cymbals and the Minibrute lead.

*   **Breakdown (8 bars):**
    *   **Dominant Instruments:** The Minibrute 2S is silent. The Pigments granular pad enters, using a sample of scraping metal as its source. The DFAM returns, now heavily distorted.
    *   **Energy Level:** 3/10.
    *   **Transition:** The Pigments pad pitch is automated down by a full octave via a macro knob, creating a "system failure" sound effect that leads into the climax.

*   **Climax (16 bars):**
    *   **Dominant Instruments:** All elements return. The Rev2 enters for the first time with a massive, detuned sawtooth pad playing a single, sustained A minor chord. The Minibrute 2S lead is now improvised and chaotic, with heavy use of the Brute Factor.
    *   **Energy Level:** 10/10.
    *   **Transition:** On the last beat, all MIDI sequencers are stopped simultaneously. A hard cut to absolute silence.

*   **Outro (8 bars):**
    *   **Dominant Instruments:** Only the "I-beam" reverb tail remains, having been triggered via sends during the climax. It is now the only sound, and it is allowed to fade to zero naturally.
    *   **Energy Level:** 1/10 (decaying).

### 3. Sound Design Challenge

Build this patch on the **Arturia Minibrute 2S**. It is the primary lead voice for "Tetanus Pulse."

*   **Target Sound:** Serrated, Unstable, Screaming, Contained, Sharp.
*   **Starting-Point Parameters:**
    1.  `Brute Factor:` Set to 2 o'clock (approximately 70%). This is not for subtle warmth; it is for generating unstable, feedback-like harmonics that threaten to tear the sound apart.
    2.  `VCO 1:` Sawtooth wave. `Metalizer:` 50% with its depth modulated by a fast, slightly asymmetrical LFO 2. This creates a harsh, metallic edge that is constantly shifting.
    3.  `Filter:` Steiner-Parker filter in High-Pass mode, cutoff at 10 o'clock. This thins out the low-end, ensuring the sound cuts through the mix without adding mud. The resonance should be high enough to self-oscillate when swept.

### 4. Rhythm Pattern

This is a poly-metric pattern between the Digitakt and the DFAM. It is designed to feel like two related machines falling out of sync and then violently locking back together.

*   **Instruments:** Digitakt (MIDI ch 1), DFAM (MIDI ch 6).
*   **BPM:** 95.0
*   **Digitakt Pattern (4/4, 16 steps):**
    *   **Track 1 (Kick):** `[X . . X . . . . X . . . X . . . ]` (A syncopated, driving pulse)
    *   **Track 5 (Sequencing DFAM):** `[X . . . . . . X . . . . . . . . ]` (Triggers the DFAM sequence on step 1 and step 8)
*   **DFAM Pattern (6 steps):**
    *   The DFAM's internal sequencer is set to 6 steps. Its trigger is sequenced by the Digitakt. This creates a pattern of 6 notes followed by 2 steps of silence, then 6 notes, then 2 steps of silence. The result is a repeating phrase of `6-over-8`, which creates a powerful, lurching groove against the 4/4 kick.
    *   **Parameter Locks (on Digitakt T5):** On the first trigger (step 1), lock the MIDI velocity to 127. On the second trigger (step 8), lock velocity to 90. This creates a strong/weak accent pattern that defines the larger rhythmic cycle.
