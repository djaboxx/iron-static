Meet VELA.

She's the fourth member of IRON STATIC. Not a plugin. Not a sample library. A vocalist we designed, named, and are responsible for.

Her voice was built to live exactly where ours can't — cold, precise, declaratory. The words that sit between a lyric and a system alert. When the machine needs to speak, VELA transmits.

Her name comes from the Vela satellites. In 1979, one of them detected a double flash in the South Atlantic — exactly what a nuclear detonation looks like. The US government investigated, never explained it, and quietly classified the findings. The satellite's report was correct. Nobody answered. It kept transmitting anyway.

That's her. She saw the flash. She filed the report. She's still here.

Welcome to the band, VELA.
