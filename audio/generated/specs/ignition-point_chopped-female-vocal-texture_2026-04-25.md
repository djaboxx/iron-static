# Audio Generation Spec: chopped female vocal texture

**Song**: ignition-point  
**Date**: 2026-04-25  

---

## GENERATION PROMPT

At 116 BPM, generate a haunting, chopped female vocal texture in an E Phrygian style. Focus on fragmented, wordless vowel sounds and breath bursts, resequenced into a cold, dehumanized yet recognizably human texture. Imagine a machine processing a ghost, creating an industrial, glitchy vocal element akin to Nine Inch Nails' textural intensity or Modeselektor's rhythmic precision in sonic manipulation. The energy should be tense, unsettling, and internal, reminiscent of Dr. Teeth's chaotic weirdness but applied to a digitally corrupted voice. Keep the sound dry with only a subtle sense of room, avoiding lush reverb or clear melodic lines.

## TECHNICAL PARAMETERS

-   **BPM:** 116.0
-   **Key centre:** E Phrygian (implied, not strictly melodic)
-   **Suggested duration:** 8-bar loop (approx. 16.5 seconds)
-   **Time signature:** 4/4
-   **Frequency focus:** Upper-mid (1kHz - 5kHz) with harmonic content extending into the high-mids (5kHz - 10kHz) for breathiness and textural sharpness.
-   **Stereo field:** Subtle stereo spread, mostly center, with occasional short, dry delays or alternating panning to create a sense of width without lushness.

## HARDWARE PARALLEL

**Instrument:** Elektron Digitakt MK1

**Key Patch Settings:**
1.  **Samples:** Load several short, isolated female vocal samples—single vowel sounds (e.g., "ah," "oh," "ee"), breath exhalations, and glottal stops. Assign these to multiple Digitakt tracks or use sample-start/end modulation on a single longer sample.
2.  **Sequencing:** Program rhythmic but fragmented trigger patterns using conditional trigs and probability for non-linear, resequenced playback. Utilize parameter locks (P-locks) extensively for sample selection, start/end points, and individual step processing.
3.  **Amp Envelope:** Fast attack (0), short decay (15-25), zero sustain, fast release (10-15) for sharp, chopped, staccato vocal fragments.
4.  **Filter:** Apply a high-pass filter (HPF) to remove low-end muddiness, or a narrow band-pass filter (BPF) to emphasize specific vocal formants. Use subtle LFO modulation on the filter cutoff to create evolving texture, with minimal resonance.
5.  **LFOs:** Apply subtle LFOs to sample pitch (fine tune for slight detuning), filter cutoff, and panning (slow, random L/R movement).
6.  **Effects:**
    *   **Delay:** A short, stereo or ping-pong delay with low feedback and a dry mix to create subtle spatialization and rhythmic echoes without washing out the sound.
    *   **Reverb:** A very small room or plate reverb with a short decay time (less than 1s) and a low wet mix, just enough to give a sense of "slight room."
    *   **Bit Reduction/Overdrive:** Use sparingly on a few individual steps via P-locks to enhance the "dehumanized" or "glitchy" aspect.
